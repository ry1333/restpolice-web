How It Works

Four simple steps.

1

Start a workout

Open Rest Police on your Apple Watch and begin your gym session.

2

Tap "Set Done"

After completing a set, tap to start your customized rest countdown.

3

Rest ends, apps shield

When the timer hits zero, selected apps on your iPhone become temporarily blocked.

4

Log next set to continue

Tap "Set Done" again to unshield apps and continue your workout.

Privacy

Your data stays yours.

Rest Police uses Apple's Screen Time APIs solely to help you focus. We never access your personal information.

You choose which apps to shield. We never select apps for you.
Rest Police does not sell your personal data. Ever.
We do not share device or app usage data with third parties.
We do not record Screen Time logs, screenshots, or screen recordings.
Workout data is stored locally and may optionally sync across your own devices.

FAQ

Common questions.

Does Rest Police read my messages or browsing history?

No. Rest Police only uses Apple's Screen Time APIs to temporarily shield apps you select. We cannot and do not read your messages, browsing history, or any content within apps.

Can I choose which apps get blocked?

Yes. You have full control over which apps are shielded. Use Apple's built-in app picker to select specific apps or entire categories. You can change your selection anytime.

What happens if my phone isn't nearby?

The Watch app works independently for timing your rest periods. App shielding commands are queued and delivered when your iPhone is reachable. Your workout data syncs automatically.

Can I disable blocking anytime?

Absolutely. You can disable app shielding entirely in Settings, use the emergency unlock button during a workout, or simply end your workout to remove all shields immediately.

Is Rest Police a parental control app?

No. Rest Police is designed for individuals managing their own focus during workouts. It uses the "Individual" authorization mode of Apple's Family Controls framework—you control your own device only.
